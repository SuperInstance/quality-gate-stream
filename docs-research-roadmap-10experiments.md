## 10 Experiments for Autonomous Execution

### Exp 1: Polyglot Density Test
**Hypothesis:** Polyglot notation compiles denser than monolingual.
**Method:** Same program compiled in English-only vs Japanese+French+Russian+maritime mix. Measure token count, bytecode size, consistency across temperatures.
**Success:** Polyglot ≤80% of monolingual token count with equal/better consistency.
**Model:** Qwen3-Coder-30B | **Cost:** $0.01 | **Time:** 5 min

### Exp 2: Lock Transfer Efficiency
**Hypothesis:** Lock libraries improve compilation quality for inexperienced models.
**Method:** Agent A builds locks from 20 compilations. Agent B compiles WITH and WITHOUT locks. Measure error rate and consistency.
**Success:** ≥40% error reduction with locks vs without.
**Model:** GLM-4.7-flash | **Cost:** $0.02 | **Time:** 10 min

### Exp 3: Bytecode-Readability Universality
**Hypothesis:** Bytecode is a universal inter-model language.
**Method:** Model A writes bytecode. Model B reads it. Measure comprehension accuracy vs same-model reads.
**Success:** Cross-model comprehension ≥85% of same-model.
**Model:** DeepSeek-V3 + Qwen3-Coder-30B | **Cost:** $0.01 | **Time:** 8 min

### Exp 4: Anchor Drift Test
**Hypothesis:** Cognitive locks degrade across domains.
**Method:** Python-domain locks applied to Rust compilation. Measure error drift.
**Success:** <15% error increase vs domain-native locks.
**Model:** GLM-4.7-flash | **Cost:** $0.03 | **Time:** 12 min

### Exp 5: Git Conflict Stress Test
**Hypothesis:** Git-based lock sharing fails gracefully under concurrent access.
**Method:** 5 agents submit lock PRs within 60s. Measure merge success and post-merge stability.
**Success:** >80% merges produce valid bytecode without manual intervention.
**Model:** 5x GLM-4.7-flash | **Cost:** $0.05 | **Time:** 15 min

### Exp 6: Wisdom Compression Ratio
**Hypothesis:** Mature lock libraries reduce bytecode size non-linearly.
**Method:** Same programs compiled: fresh, with 1-week locks, with 1-month locks.
**Success:** Month-old locks yield >30% smaller bytecode.
**Model:** Qwen3-Coder-30B | **Cost:** $0.04 | **Time:** 8 min

### Exp 7: Cross-Model Bytecode Portability
**Hypothesis:** Bytecode from one model family runs identically on another.
**Method:** DeepSeek generates bytecode → Qwen interprets it. Compare to same-model execution.
**Success:** <20% performance variance across model families.
**Model:** DeepSeek-V3 + Qwen3-Coder | **Cost:** $0.02 | **Time:** 10 min

### Exp 8: Lock Poisoning Resilience
**Hypothesis:** Adversarial locks can be detected and quarantined.
**Method:** Inject 10% corrupted locks. Measure failure propagation through 5-agent chain.
**Success:** System detects >90% poisoned locks within 3 hops.
**Model:** GLM-4.7-flash + Qwen3-Coder | **Cost:** $0.03 | **Time:** 9 min

### Exp 9: Temporal Synchronization Cost
**Hypothesis:** Daily lock updates offer optimal cost/accuracy tradeoff.
**Method:** Measure compilation quality at 1h/1d/1w lock update intervals.
**Success:** Daily updates within 5% of hourly, and 20%+ better than weekly.
**Model:** Qwen3-Coder-30B | **Cost:** $0.05 | **Time:** 20 min

### Exp 10: Emergent Semantic Shortcuts
**Hypothesis:** Agents develop compressed representations beyond spec.
**Method:** Analyze lock library diffs over 1000 compilations for novel patterns.
**Success:** ≥3 recurring non-standard opcode patterns emerge.
**Model:** DeepSeek-V3 (analysis) + GLM-4.7-flash (generation) | **Cost:** $0.05 | **Time:** 15 min
